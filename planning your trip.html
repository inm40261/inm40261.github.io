Planning your Trip

Destination

Things to consider:

  1. Are you a student?
    • Look into study abroad options for university credit
    • Seek international internships in your desired career
    • Find research opportunities with faculty or as a graduate student
  2. Budget
    • Compare flight prices to different countries you're interested in
    • Consider how often you plan on traveling within and outside of the desired country
    • Research transportation options and fares for that travel
    • Know the currency exchange rate and figure out the amount of spending money needed
  3. Housing
    • Consider a home stay if you're traveling with a program of some sort
    • AirBNB has options to live in a home with the local homeowners
    • Choose a hotel or rent a house in a non-tourist area
  4. Health and Safety
    • Click here to check the CDC Travelers' Health page for any vaccinations you may need before travel
    • Visit your doctor or a travel clinic to recieve preventative medicines in case you get sick while abroad
    • Know the health risks such as water safety before traveling to desired country
    • Click here to register in STEP for support from the U.S Embassy in
      case of emergency while abroad

What do you wish to gain from your trip?

In order to maximize your experience and choose the best destination for you, you should first evaluate your goals for the trip. Do you hope to learn a brand new language, perfect one that you have been studying, or are you more comfortable in an English-speaking country? Would you rather be in a college town, the city, or the countryside? Are you more attracted to the arts, history, or nature? These are just a few of the questions that you need to ask yourself when you begin researching destinations. Once you choose a country, you need to decide which city or cities you want to stay in and have a few ideas on what you might do while you're there. Then you can assess the accessibility and possible costs associated with travelling both within the country and to neighboring nations.
