Packing your Bags

For your suitcase:
Now that you have picked your destination,
research the climate during the time of year
you plan to travel. Pack weather and culturally
appropriate clothing. I suggest googling how
locals typically dress to gain a better idea of
what pieces to pack. Don't overpack! Pack
your suitcase and then take out half, only
keeping the essentials. Depending on where
you are going and your housing arrangements,
you may not have access to elevators or
washer/dryer machines. Pack clothes that you
can mix and match for more use. If you will be
staying for a long period of time, only pack the
toiletries that are necessary for a few weeks.
Once you get acquainted with the new area,
you can buy more soap, shampoo, sunscreen,
etc. Be sure to research what may not be
readily available to you while abroad. Don't
forget to pack chargers for your devices and
electrical adapters if necessary. Also bring a
lock to secure your things if you will be
sharing a living space with others.
For your carry-on bag:
Pack any medications that you are traveling
with in your carry-on. Have all important
documents such as your wallet, passport,
flight information, and the address and
directions to your final destination/housing.
If you purchase travel insurance, always
have that card handy as well. Just in case
your luggage is lost or delayed in
arriving to your final destination, have an
extra set of clothes and a toothbrush in
your carry-on. Of course, pack
entertainment for the flight such as
a book or magazine. Also have a few
snacks and a bottle of water for a long
trip. Before boarding the plane, stop by
a currency exhange stand and get a small
amount of money exchanged so you will
have some cash upon landing just in case.
Also be prepared that some countries
charge an entry fee that must be paid at
customs before being allowed in the
country, so make sure you have enough
cash to pay this.
